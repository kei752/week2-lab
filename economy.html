Global Supply Chain Disruptions and Economic Recovery

The COVID-19 pandemic and geopolitical tensions (such as the war in Ukraine) have caused major disruptions in global supply chains. These disruptions have led to shortages, inflation, and slower economic recovery. This topic explores how countries and industries are adapting to these challenges, what long-term changes might be made to supply chains, and the implications for global trade and economic growth.

The Future of Work and Automation

As technology, particularly artificial intelligence and robotics, continues to advance, the nature of work is changing. This topic examines the impact of automation on various industries, the potential for job displacement, and the role of reskilling and upskilling programs. It also explores how automation could affect economic inequality and the future of labor markets globally.

Cryptocurrency and Its Impact on the Global Economy

Cryptocurrencies and decentralized finance (DeFi) have emerged as new economic forces. This topic delves into the growing influence of cryptocurrencies on traditional financial systems, their potential to disrupt banking and investment sectors, and the regulatory challenges posed by their rise. It also considers the environmental impact of cryptocurrency mining and the risks and opportunities presented by digital currencies in both developed and developing economies.
